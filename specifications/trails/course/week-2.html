What to expect from the webinar

Before and After

Before each webinar, each course participant will receive an e-mail summary of what each participant can expect from the webinar, and what they should have prepared. After class, notes will be distributed so that you may share the content with your colleagues, and a recording of the webinar will be available in the Code for America library.

Given the brief duration of the course, it is mandatory that at least one staffperson from your agency be present for each webinar.


During the Webinar

The webinar will be broadcast via a Google Hangout, and facilitated by Garret Jacobs, Peer Network Coordinator at Code for America. Jereme Monteau will be present to provide technical support.

Time: TBD

Link: TBD

Duration: Approximately 1 hour

Webinar Agenda

  • Review Week 1 Coursework

    • Brief review of each agency's data.
    • Feedback session about Converter and Validator tools.
    • Discussion about data quality and iterative improvement.

  • In-depth overview of existing OpenTrails tools, apps and vendors

    • Open Data Portals
    • OuterSpatial and ESRI
    • Apps and Embeds
    • Developer tools

  • The Process of Publishing Open Data: Session 2

    • Review progress identifying internal stakeholders.
    • Discussion of blockers and resources.

Coursework for Week 3

  • Complete an Agency Status Report

    • Describe the current status of your data
    • Describe the current status of your open data infrastructure.
    • Describe the current status of your internal buy-in
    • Describe which outcomes, tools or apps your agency is most interested in.

  • Complete the first draft of your OpenTrails Sustainability Plans

    • Code for America will provide the template, touching on:
      • Roles & Responsibilities
      • Your likely publishing mechanism
      • How you will likely handle and respond to feedback
      • How frequently you will update your data, by what method, and how changes to the process might be handled.
