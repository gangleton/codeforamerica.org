What to expect from the webinar

Before and After

Before each webinar, each course participant will receive an e-mail summary of what each participant can expect from the webinar, and what they should have prepared. After class, notes will be distributed so that you may share the content with your colleagues, and a recording of the webinar will be available in the Code for America library.

Given the brief duration of the course, it is mandatory that at least one staffperson from your agency be present for each webinar.


During the Webinar

The webinar will be broadcast via a Google Hangout, and facilitated by Garret Jacobs, Peer Network Coordinator at Code for America. Jereme Monteau will be present to provide technical support.

Time: TBD

Link: TBD

Duration: Approximately 1 hour

Webinar Agenda

  • Review Sustainability Plans and Goals

    • Technical infrastructure
    • Data freshness and changes
    • Roles and responsibilities
    • Next steps for implementation

  • The Process of Publishing Open Data: Session 3

    • Report outs on internal-buy in.
    • Identifying a tech vendor or internal support for future troubleshooting
    • How to market the data to your civic hacking and local tech communities
    • Schedule follow ups with Code for America.

Final Coursework

  • Publish your data

    • Publish your data based on your agencies current status.
    • Publish your data with your sustainability plan in mind.
    • Implement the app or embed that can consume your data, or identify the use your agency will pursue.

  • Participate in a 1:1 Feedback session with Code for America.

    • Help us evaluate the value of the OpenTrails course.
    • Identify ways it can be enhanced for the next cohort.
    • Review what assistance your agency requires to continue publishing OpenTrails data.
