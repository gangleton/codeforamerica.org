Procurement Working Group FAQ

Code for America is convening a small, select cohort of highly committed cities to work closely with on improving procurement.

What does my government need to do as part of the working group?

To join the Working Group, you'll need to identify two designated staff members from your municipality to participate — one from a technology or innovation-focused department, and one from the procurement or legal department. Both participants will be expected to:

  • Demonstrate substantial commitment to improving procurement in their municipality, and generate buy-in with relevant stakeholders and leadership.
  • Participate in 2-3 preparatory phone calls with CfA staff and/or advisors before September 2014 to help us understand needs and priorities around procurement in your municipality
  • Join 1-2 collaborative virtual roundtables with other cities to share ideas, give feedback, and learn from each other's approaches.
  • Engage and facilitate collaboration with other relevant stakeholders within their government.
  • Attend the CfA Summit (September 23-25 in San Francisco) and participate in 2-3 hours of working sessions on procurement

These are the minimum participation expectations, and we hope to work closely with our working group partners to go above and beyond what's listed here.

What are the benefits of participating?

As a member of the working group, participants will:

  • Learn about best practices and cutting edge procurement improvements from around the country, and connect directly with experts who have first-hand experience.
  • Get individualized support from Code for America staff and advisors in determining an action plan that fits your city’s unique needs, strengths, and priorities.
  • Get free admission to the Code for America Summit and participate in a series of collaborative workshops and working sessions to put your plan into action.
  • Gain national recognition as part of a pioneering group working on this important issue -- including recognition at the Summit, features in CfA materials, and other opportunities.
